Background

Ever since I can remember I’ve been obsessed with two things, theatre and computers. In elementary school I would learn basic coding on the drag-and-drop coding websites and act in the school’s production of Peter Pan.

As I matured into a teenager I merged my computer science and artistic appreciations by joining the technical side of theatre programming lighting instruments, and learning how to manage large-scale power systems in the context of theatrical systems.

Throughout my high school career I diversified my computer science experience by learning how to program Microsoft Office macros, websites, and object-oriented programs in Java. Concurrently, I developed my electrical experience with lighting and scenery systems with wireless communication protocols built into wood, metal, and plastic set pieces.

Throughout all of this experience I biggest thing I learned is how insecure our systems are. Whether its a website or a lighting instrument, almost everything in our modern world is vulnerable to cyber attacks. At Georgia Institute of Technology, I am studying the cybersecurity thread of computer engineering to better understand the vulnerabilities in our digital devices. I’m actively seeking a co-op in the cybersecurity industry to better secure our digital world.
